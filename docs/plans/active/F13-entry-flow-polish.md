# F13 Entry Flow Polish

## Goal

Reduce confusion in the public entry flow so the home page clearly sells FIC as the product and the signup path feels like a short, calm commercial handoff rather than a brand explanation exercise.

## Why Now

- the company brand layer is now in place, but the home page is over-explaining the relationship between North Star and FIC
- the current entry flow still makes merchants work too hard to understand what to click next
- signup copy is directionally correct but still carries too much narrative for a simple first step

## Scope

- simplify the home page so it is product-first and company-backed
- reduce competing CTAs and explanatory copy on the home page
- make the signup step read as a simple account/shop creation handoff into billing and the workspace
- keep North Star visible as support/credibility, not as the main thing being sold on `/`
- add targeted proof for the updated entry flow

## Planned Outcomes

- the home page answers `what is this`, `who is it for`, and `what do I do next` quickly
- FIC is the clear product CTA
- North Star reads as the company/support layer, not as a competing product
- signup feels lighter and more direct

## Exit Criteria

- `F13` spec exists with explicit requirements and acceptance
- `F12` moves to completed plans
- home page copy and layout are materially simpler and more product-led
- signup copy is shorter and clearer about what happens next
- targeted tests and validator pass
