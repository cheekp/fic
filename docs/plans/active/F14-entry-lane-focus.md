# F14 Entry Lane Focus

## Goal

Sharpen the public entry flow so the home page reads as one clear product lane into signup, with the company layer present but quiet.

## Why Now

- `F13` improved the entry flow, but the page can still feel like it is explaining both a company and a product at once
- the current home page still has more supporting narrative than a fast-moving merchant needs
- signup and billing should feel like one clean commercial handoff, not separate explanatory surfaces

## Scope

- reduce narrative and competing signals on the home page
- keep one clear product CTA and one quiet account path
- quiet the consultancy/company layer further on the home page
- tighten signup and billing copy so they feel like a single simple onboarding lane
- keep the rest of the product hierarchy unchanged

## Planned Outcomes

- the home page sells FIC quickly
- North Star remains visible as backing/support, but does not compete with the product message
- signup and billing feel lighter and more coherent as a sequence
- the public side feels calmer and more intentional

## Exit Criteria

- `F14` spec exists with requirements and acceptance
- `F13` moves to completed plans
- home page narrative is reduced further without losing clarity
- signup and billing copy align as one flow
- validator and tests pass
