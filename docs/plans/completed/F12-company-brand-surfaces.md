# F12 Company Brand Surfaces

## Goal

Apply Jo's company-brand guidance to the acquisition and support layer of the product so the home page, account flows, and support chrome feel like North Star Customer Solutions while the merchant workspace stays merchant-owned.

## Why Now

- the workspace hierarchy is now good enough that the next confusion point is brand layering, not programme structure
- the home page and account/support surfaces still feel like generic product scaffolding rather than a credible consultancy-backed product
- Jo has now supplied company brand guidance that is strong enough to shape these surfaces without guessing
- we need to make the company layer feel intentional without bleeding it into merchant-owned programme and Wallet experiences

## Scope

- use `docs/business/brand-guidlines.txt` as the source for company-layer tone, palette, and messaging
- update the home page to reflect the North Star company position while keeping the FIC signup CTA clear
- update account and recovery pages so they feel like branded support surfaces rather than isolated form stubs
- update merchant utility chrome and agent copy to feel like company support rather than generic platform scaffolding
- add simple routed stubs for consultancy, billing, and account support where needed
- keep the merchant workspace itself merchant-owned and do not replace merchant branding with company branding

## Planned Outcomes

- the home page reads as a credible company-backed product entry point
- the support layer feels calmer, more strategic, and more premium
- merchants can tell the difference between company support surfaces and their own branded workspace
- support, consultancy, and account/billing utilities feel intentional rather than placeholder-only

## Exit Criteria

- `F12` spec exists with explicit requirements and acceptance
- `F11` has moved to completed plans
- the home page uses North Star-inspired tone and visual treatment without relying on the supplied logo image
- account, billing, consultancy, and support surfaces align to the company brand layer
- merchant utility chrome reads as company support rather than primary product navigation
- tests and validator pass after the company surface changes
