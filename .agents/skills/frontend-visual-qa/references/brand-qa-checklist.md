# Brand QA Checklist

Use this checklist with `docs/business/brand-guidlines.txt`.

## North Star brand fit

- The page should feel strategic, calm, and commercially credible.
- Avoid loud startup energy, gimmicky gradients, washed-out text, or “marketing agency” styling.
- The first viewport should feel closer to premium consultancy than promo microsite.

## Palette and contrast

- Primary direction should respect the brand palette:
  - Deep Navy `#0F1B2A`
  - Warm Gold `#C8A96A`
  - Soft Ivory `#F5F3EF`
  - Slate Grey `#4A4F55`
- Body copy must remain legible against its background.
- Headlines must not disappear into glow, haze, or low-contrast backgrounds.
- Primary CTAs must remain clearly visible and readable at a glance.

## Typography and hierarchy

- Headline should feel authoritative and editorial, not decorative.
- Supporting copy should be readable without squinting.
- Above-the-fold hierarchy should be obvious in this order:
  - brand/context
  - headline
  - supporting copy
  - primary CTA
- Copy should describe the decision or action on the page, not narrate the interface.
- If a side panel or support card does not add new information, it should be removed.
- Repeated headings such as `What this step affects`, `Used for`, `Approach`, `Operating model`, or other meta labels should be treated as likely filler and challenged.

## Imagery and composition

- Imagery should feel like real retail/customer environments, not generic corporate stock.
- The hero must not split attention so evenly that the headline loses dominance.
- Decorative effects should support composition, not wash it out.

## Failure patterns to flag

- Low-contrast white text on pale or glowing backgrounds
- CTA buttons that visually disappear
- Over-soft gradients that flatten hierarchy
- A headline that is large but unreadable
- Navigation or utility elements stronger than the core proposition
- Layouts that feel generic, trendy, or louder than “calm authority”
- Copy that sounds respectable but says little
- Panels that restate the form or roadmap without adding new information
- “Consultancy” language used as decoration rather than concrete meaning
